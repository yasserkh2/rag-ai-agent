# Communication Services

## Service Overview
Communication Services supports patient-facing reminders, scheduling coordination, follow-up workflows, and related communication processes that keep interactions timely and consistent. It is designed for practices that want fewer communication gaps, better follow-through, and a smoother experience across routine touchpoints.

## What This Service Usually Includes
- Reminder workflows for appointments or follow-up needs
- Scheduling coordination support
- Communication process improvement across common touchpoints
- Follow-up workflow design or cleanup
- Better consistency in outbound and responsive communication

## Common Practice Scenarios
- Patients are missing reminders or receiving inconsistent follow-up.
- Scheduling communication is fragmented across channels or staff members.
- Leadership wants a more dependable communication rhythm.
- The practice needs stronger coordination between scheduling and follow-up.

## Information To Collect During Intake
- Practice name and communication or operations contact
- Current reminder and follow-up process
- Main pain points in scheduling coordination
- Channels in use such as phone, email, or text
- Volume expectations and urgency
- Desired improvement goals

## How The Chatbot Should Respond
Provide a concise overview, then ask whether the user is focused on reminders, scheduling coordination, or follow-up workflows. If the user wants help moving forward, capture the current communication gaps and desired outcomes before routing onward.

## Escalation Guidance
Escalate when the user needs custom workflow design, shares privacy-sensitive communication issues, or asks for case-specific guidance tied to current patient operations. Human support is also appropriate when the request crosses into broader operational redesign.

## Example Customer Questions
- What does communication services include?
- Can this help with reminders and follow-up?
- What should we prepare before speaking with the team?
- When should communication issues be escalated?
