# Medical Auditing

## Service Overview
Medical Auditing focuses on documentation quality, coding accuracy, billing process review, and compliance risk awareness. Practices usually seek this service when they want an objective review of how documentation and coding are being handled before issues become larger operational or regulatory problems.

## What This Service Usually Includes
- Documentation review for completeness and supportability
- Coding quality review and pattern checks
- Billing process accuracy assessment
- Identification of compliance-sensitive gaps
- Recommendations for improvement and corrective follow-up

## Common Practice Scenarios
- A practice wants a proactive compliance review.
- Leadership has noticed coding inconsistencies across providers or teams.
- Billing outcomes suggest documentation may not support submitted coding.
- The organization wants an external perspective before internal risk increases.

## Information To Collect During Intake
- Practice name and compliance or operations contact
- Main audit concerns or review objectives
- Relevant specialties or provider groups
- Documentation or coding areas that need attention
- Time period or sample scope under discussion
- Desired timeline and urgency

## How The Chatbot Should Respond
Provide a clear description of the service, then determine whether the user needs documentation review, coding review, or a broader audit conversation. Keep the response informational unless the user wants to proceed with consultation or escalation.

## Escalation Guidance
Escalate when the user requests interpretation of a specific compliance issue, shares sensitive patient or billing details, or asks for judgment on a live audit matter. Human review is preferred whenever the conversation moves from education to case-specific risk analysis.
